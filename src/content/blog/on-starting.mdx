The hardest part of any project is the first commit. After that, the
inertia flips: you're editing something rather than facing a blank page.

A few things that help me past the first commit:

- Pick the smallest thing that could be called "done" and ship that.
- Let the boring scaffolding be boring — no need to invent.
- Reserve taste for what's *in* the thing, not how it was built.
